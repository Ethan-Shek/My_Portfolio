Pudgie's Redesign

Pudgie's Redesign


Role: Designer & Brand Developer (Independent Project)
Tools: Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, InDesign
Focus: Visual Identity · Typography · Physical Prototyping

Project Overview

Project Overview

This project was a complete logo and brand identity redesign for Pudgie’s Pizzeria, a local restaurant known for its welcoming, homestyle atmosphere. My goal was to modernize the logo while keeping its approachable and family-oriented character.

Design Approach

Design Approach

I approached this project as both a branding and UX challenge. Creating a cohesive identity that worked digitally, in print, and in physical space.
✦ Research & Concepting: Started with competitor analysis and mood mapping to capture key brand traits: warm, bold, approachable, and local.
✦ Sketching & Iteration: Explored over 20 logo sketches, experimenting with text-image integration and typographic form to find a simple, memorable identity.
✦ Typography & Color: Selected a rounded, bold typeface to reflect friendliness and trust. Used a warm red-gold palette reminiscent of classic pizza shop interiors.
✦ Brand Application: Designed supporting materials: a menu layout, business card, and letterhead. Ensuring consistent hierarchy and visual balance across touchpoints.
✦ Physical Prototype: Constructed a framed, physical version of the menu to simulate an in-store display. This step helped evaluate real-world readability, color contrast, and user interaction from a customer’s perspective.

Deliverables

Deliverables

✦ Redesigned logo system (color and monochrome versions).
✦ Menu and brand collateral redesign with print-ready layouts.
✦ Physical prototype frame showcasing the new menu in real-world context.
